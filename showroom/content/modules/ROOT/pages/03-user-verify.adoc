= Verify Certificates

In this section you will verify a certificate's authenticity using the public verification portal — no login required.

== Step 1: Open the Cert Portal

Navigate to the *Cert Portal*: {cert_portal_url}

This is the public-facing verification interface. Anyone (employers, universities, students) can use it to verify a certificate.

== Step 2: Verify by Certificate ID

. Paste the *Certificate ID* you copied in the previous section into the search field.
. Click *Verify*.
. The portal queries the `verify-api`, which reads directly from the blockchain ledger.
. You should see the *public* certificate details:
** Student name
** Course name
** Issuing organization
** Issue and expiry dates
** Status (VALID / REVOKED / EXPIRED)

NOTE: As an anonymous user, you will *not* see the grade or degree fields. These private fields are only visible to the certificate owner when logged in.

== Step 3: Try an Invalid ID

. Enter a random string (e.g., `invalid-cert-123`).
. Click *Verify*.
. The portal should show *Certificate Not Found* — confirming that only legitimate certificates exist on the blockchain.

== How It Works

The verification flow:

. *cert-portal* (frontend) sends the certificate ID to the *verify-api*.
. *verify-api* queries the Fabric peer using the `evaluateTransaction` gateway call.
. The peer reads the certificate from its CouchDB state database.
. The result is returned — no blockchain write occurs (read-only query).

[TIP]
====
Verification is a read-only operation — it does not create a new blockchain transaction. This makes it fast and free of ordering/consensus overhead.
====

== Step 4: Student Login — View Full Transcript

As a student, you can log in to see your full transcript including private fields like grade and degree.

. On the Cert Portal, click *Login* (top-right).
. You are redirected to the *Central Keycloak* login page.
. Enter the student email: `student01@techpulse.demo`
. Keycloak detects the `@techpulse.demo` domain and auto-redirects to *TechPulse Keycloak* (no manual IDP selection).
. Enter password: `student`
. After login, click *My Transcript*.
. You now see all your certificates — including *grade* and *degree* fields on your own certificates.

=== Privacy Enforcement

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Scenario | What you see

| Anonymous verification | Public info only (status, name, course, org, dates)
| Logged in — your own cert | Full details including grade and degree
| Logged in — someone else's cert | Public info only (grade/degree hidden)
| Admin (Course Manager) | Full details for all org certificates
|===

This privacy enforcement is applied server-side by the `verify-api`. The JWT email claim is matched against the certificate's `studentID` to determine ownership.
